For Universities.

Common questions from our university partners on student verification, admissions coordination, Clearing support, and how our consultancy works.

Our partner placement desk is highly active. We aim to respond with fully verified, pre-screened applicant profiles matching your department's specific entry criteria within 24 hours of enquiry.
Every student profile is verified by our compliance team. We collect original exam certificates, calculate verified UCAS tariff points, and gather professional/academic references before student introduction.
Yes. We work in tandem with admissions offices to provide verified, high-quality matches for both standard entry cycles and fast-tracked late admissions.
Each applicant undergoes comprehensive academic mapping, a mock interview, and statement review sessions conducted by former university admissions tutors.
Yes, our advisory team operates an extended helpline during the August/September UCAS Clearing cycle to match student candidates with open course spaces instantly.
Absolutely. We segment our candidates by specialism (STEM, Law, Medicine, Humanities) so you can coordinate outreach with relevant academic departments.
If a placed student officially withdraws from their course before the university's standard autumn census date, partnership commission terms are fully waived or credited.
Yes, our postgraduate division handles specialist headhunting and placement for MBA, MSc, MA, and doctoral-level programs.
We are advocates for widening access. We work closely with student support teams to ensure any accessibility, disability, or learning support requirements are pre-registered and structured.
For professional programs requiring Enhanced DBS checks (e.g. Medicine, Nursing, Education), we verify the student's eligibility status prior to introducing their application.

We support students across all regions of England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland, mapping qualifications to entry requirements at any UK university.
Our international admissions desks verify global transcripts using UK ENIC guidelines, converting local grades into UCAS tariff equivalents.
