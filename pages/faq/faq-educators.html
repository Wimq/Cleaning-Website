For Students.

Everything you need to know about applying for a university placement, English language requirements, and how our admission pathways work.

Yes — IELTS (or an equivalent English language test such as TOEFL or PTE) is the primary requirement we look for. Most of our partner universities require a minimum IELTS score of 6.0 overall. We will advise you on the exact score needed for your chosen course and university.
Requirements vary by course and institution, but a score of 6.0–6.5 overall (with no band below 5.5) is typically accepted. Some courses such as Medicine or Law may require 6.5–7.0. Our advisors will match you to universities whose IELTS threshold fits your result.
Yes. We work with students regardless of their prior academic background. As long as you hold a valid IELTS score, we can explore suitable pathways — including foundation years and direct entry — to get you into the right university program.
No — there is no age limit. We welcome applications from school leavers, mature students, and working professionals alike. As long as you meet the IELTS requirement, we can find the right university route for you.
After a brief registration, we conduct a consultation to understand your goals and IELTS result. We then match you to the best-fit courses, optimise your personal statement, submit your application, and liaise directly with admissions offices to secure your place.
Yes, the majority of undergraduate applications go through UCAS. Our admissions specialists guide you step-by-step in establishing your UCAS profile, choosing the right course codes, and submitting a strong application.
Yes, many of our partner universities offer January intakes in addition to the traditional September start date, giving you the flexibility to begin study when you are ready.
Clearing opens in July/August for students who do not yet hold university offers. We negotiate directly with universities on your behalf during this cycle to lock in late places quickly.
Our advisors will schedule a call to compare course structures, student satisfaction ratings, and locations to help you decide on your Firm and Insurance choices.
Yes, our postgraduate placement desk supports MSc, MA, and MBA candidates, helping optimise applications for professional and executive programs.
Yes, we partner with multiple universities that offer flexible part-time, evening, and hybrid online learning options to suit working students.
Yes! If you refer a fellow student who successfully registers and enrols in a university placement through our consultancy, we offer a £50 book voucher as a thank you.
