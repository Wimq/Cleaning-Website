1. Definitions & Interpretation

1.1. In these Terms of Business ("Terms"), the following definitions apply:

  • "The Company" means Kensington Ivy Ltd, a private limited company (Companies House No. 17167406).
  • "The Institution" means the university, college, or higher education provider to which a Student is introduced or placed.
  • "Student" means the individual applicant introduced or referred by the Company for enrollment at the Institution.
  • "Introduction Fee" or "Placement Commission" means the fee payable by the Institution to the Company upon successful student enrollment and census clearance.
  • "Enrollment Date" means the date the Student officially registers and clears the Institution's census date.

2. The Contract

2.1. These Terms govern the introduction and referral of Students to the Institution by the Company and represent the complete agreement between the parties.

2.2. Acceptance of a Student's academic profile, transcripts, portfolio details, or the official registration and enrollment of a Student referred by the Company shall constitute unconditional acceptance of these Terms.

2.3. No variation to these Terms shall be valid unless approved in writing by a Director of the Company.

3. Institution Obligations

3.1. The Institution retains full discretion and responsibility over admissions decisions. The Institution shall evaluate Student suitability for enrollment in accordance with its academic entry standards.

3.2. The Institution shall comply with all statutory obligations, including Higher Education regulations, the Office for Students (OfS) framework, and Student Finance England requirements.

4. Information Supply

4.1. The Company shall pre-screen and verify the Student's qualification certificates, statement transcripts, and reference credentials, providing a complete and audit-ready admissions file to the Institution.

5. Commission Fees

5.1. The Institution agrees to pay the Company's placement commission charges as specified in the pre-agreed Partnership Schedule for each successfully placed Student.

5.2. The Institution shall confirm student enrollment status and census clearance dates to the Company upon request.

6. Payment Terms & Interest

6.1. Invoices are payable within 30 days of the Student's census clearance date. The Company reserves the right to charge interest on overdue accounts at a rate of 8% above the Bank of England base rate per annum.

7. Placement Commission

7.1. Standard Placement Rate: Upon successful enrollment and census clearance of a Student introduced by the Company, the Institution shall pay a Placement Commission of 21% of the Student's first-year gross tuition fees or as pre-specified in the Partnership Schedule.

7.2. This commission applies regardless of whether the Student's registration is processed via UCAS direct entry or direct institutional portals.

8. Retention Guarantee

8.1. Should a placed Student withdraw from their course within the first 12 weeks of their first term, the Company will provide a refund or credit of the commission fee as follows:

  • Withdraws Weeks 1 - 4: 100% Refund
  • Withdraws Weeks 5 - 8: 50% Refund
  • Withdraws Weeks 9 - 12: 25% Refund

8.2. The Retention Guarantee is contingent upon the invoice being paid within 30 days and the withdrawal not being due to course cancellation or institutional failure.

9. Direct Applications

9.1. If a student introduced by the Company is enrolled directly by the Institution bypassing the referral path within the Relevant Period, the standard placement commission remains payable.

9.2. The Relevant Period is 12 months from the date of the initial Student profile introduction to the Institution.

10. Exclusivity Period

10.1. Introductions and academic profile files submitted by the Company shall remain active and exclusive for referral fee purposes for a period of 12 continuous months.

11. Offer Rejections

11.1. The Institution may reject any Student profile introduced by the Company, provided written notification of rejection and the academic reasoning is supplied within 14 working days of receipt.

12. Student Deferrals

12.1. If a Student who has been offered a place defers entry to the subsequent academic term or year, the placement commission remains payable to the Company upon successful enrollment in that subsequent term.

13. Widening Participation

13.1. Both parties agree to collaborate to support student social mobility and the Institution's Access and Participation Plan (APP) targets to widen university entry to disadvantaged communities.

14. Safeguarding & Welfare

14.1. For professional programs (e.g. Health, Medicine, Teaching), the Company will support the pre-screening of necessary DBS clearances and eligibility checks as requested by the Institution.

14.2. The Company shall process and retain student records securely, complying with all university safeguarding and Prevent Duty guidelines.

15. Liability & Indemnity

15.1. The Company accepts no liability for any loss, academic failure, or expense arising from the Student's behavior or performance during their enrollment. The Institution shall indemnify the Company against any related claims.

16. Confidentiality

16.1. The Institution shall keep confidential all personal, academic, and contact details regarding introduced Students, and shall not disclose such data to any third party without written consent.

17. Data Protection (UK GDPR)

17.1. Both parties act as independent Data Controllers and shall process personal student data in full compliance with the Data Protection Act 2018 and the UK GDPR.

18. Intellectual Property

18.1. All copyright and other intellectual property rights created in the Student's academic submissions and coursework shall belong exclusively to the Student and/or the Institution.

19. Anti-Bribery & Criminal Finances

19.1. Both parties shall comply with the Bribery Act 2010 and the Criminal Finances Act 2017 (Prevention of Tax Evasion).

20. CMA Consumer Compliance

20.1. Both parties agree to comply with Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) higher education guidance, ensuring Students are provided with accurate, transparent, and non-misleading information regarding courses and fees.

20.2. The Company will ensure all marketing materials align with the Institution's approved prospectus and fee schedules.

21. Force Majeure

21.1. Neither party shall be liable for failures caused by events beyond their reasonable control (e.g., pandemic disruptions, natural disasters, or national emergencies).

22. Governing Law & Jurisdiction

22.1. These Terms are governed by the laws of England and Wales and are subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Courts of England and Wales.
