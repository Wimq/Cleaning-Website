1. Who We Are

1.1. This Privacy Policy is issued by KENSINGTON IVY LTD ("Kensington Ivy", "we", "us", "our"), a private limited company incorporated in England and Wales. Our statutory details are:

  • Registered Name: KENSINGTON IVY LTD
  • Companies House Number: 17167406
  • SIC Code: 85600 — Education Support Services
  • Registered Office: 453 South End Road, Hornchurch, England, RM12 5NX
  • General Enquiries: info@kensingtonivy.co.uk | 020 7444 0009
  • Data & Compliance Enquiries: compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk
  • ICO Registration Number: ZC148985

1.2. Kensington Ivy is a university admissions and student placement consultancy. We assist student applicants in securing higher education course placements and partner with universities to coordinate student referrals and enrollments.

1.3. For the purposes of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018), Kensington Ivy acts as the Data Controller in respect of all personal data processed pursuant to this Policy.

1.4. We align our data processing with UK Higher Education guidelines, the Care Act 2014, and Office for Students (OfS) frameworks. Our data processing activities necessarily reflect and give effect to these guidelines.

1.5. We have appointed an internal Compliance Lead who is responsible for overseeing data protection compliance across our operations. Any queries about our data protection practices should be directed to compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk.

2. Personal Data We Collect

2.1. The categories of personal data we collect depend on your relationship with us. We collect data about three groups of individuals:

A — Students & Applicants

  • Identity Data: Full legal name, preferred name, date of birth, gender, and nationality.
  • Contact Data: Home address, email address, telephone number(s).
  • Right to Study Data: Passport, share code, or other documents evidencing your legal entitlement to study in the United Kingdom.
  • Academic Data: qualification certificates, transcript records, predicted and final grades, personal statements, reference letters, and creative portfolios (where applicable).
  • Financial Data: Student Loan eligibility status, tuition fee funding details, and bank details for advisory fees or maintenance loan distributions.
  • Admissions Vetting Data: Academic reference verifications, English language proficiency test reports, and criminal record disclosures (where required for professional course pathways like Medicine or Teaching).

B — Partner Universities & Institutions

  • Organisation Data: Institution name, address, and type.
  • Contact Data: Name, job title, email address, and direct telephone number of authorised admissions contacts (e.g., Admissions Officers, Registry staff, Outreach coordinators).
  • Contractual Data: Partnership agreements, commission rates, and enrollment report files.

C — Website Visitors

  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, pages visited, time of visit, and referral source. See our Cookie Policy for details.

2.2. We collect personal data directly from you (when you register, consult with us, or submit forms), from third parties (referees, exam boards, universities), and automatically via our website.

2.3. Where the law or university entry rules require us to collect certain data and you do not provide it, we will not be able to register you or assist in your placement.

3. Special Category Data & Criminal Records Data

3.1. By the nature of our business, we are required to process data that falls within the definition of Special Category Data under Article 9 UK GDPR, and data relating to criminal convictions and offences under Article 10 UK GDPR and Schedule 1 of the DPA 2018. This includes:

  • Criminal records data obtained via the Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS checks) for students applying to professional courses (such as Medicine, Nursing, or Teaching).
  • Health and disability data disclosed by students to arrange reasonable adjustments, university accommodation, accessibility requirements during application, or to apply for the Disabled Students' Allowance (DSA).
  • Biometric identity data processed for digital identity and Right to Study/Student Visa checks.

3.2. We process these categories of data strictly to the extent required for university admissions. We do not process Special Category Data for commercial or marketing purposes.

4. Purposes for Which We Use Your Data

4.1. We use personal data for the following specific purposes:

  • Registering and maintaining your student or university portal account.
  • Conducting qualifications audits, UCAS tariff mapping, reference checks, and verification procedures.
  • Matching students to suitable courses and presenting student admissions files to partner institutions.
  • Assisting students with Student Finance SFE application procedures and tuition fee loan mapping.
  • Tracking student enrollment status, census dates, and partnership commission fees.
  • Responding to enquiries, complaints, and regulatory files.
  • Improving our website and advisory services through anonymised analytics data.
  • Direct Marketing — Course Alerts & Updates: We may send registered applicants relevant course alerts, deadline reminders, and admissions updates. You may opt out of these communications at any time by emailing compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk.

5. Lawful Basis for Processing

5.1. We rely on the following lawful bases under Article 6 UK GDPR:

  • Performance of a Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): Where processing is necessary to perform our contract with you, or to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract — for example, registering you as an applicant and arranging placements.
  • Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): Where processing is required to comply with statutory obligations — for example, visa verification and financial reporting.
  • Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): Where processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights — for example, business administration, IT security, and direct marketing of relevant courses to registered applicants.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) & Art. 9(2)(a)): Where we rely on your explicit consent — for example, for handling sensitive Special Category Data (such as health information) or sharing your portfolio with third parties.

6. Who We Share Your Data With

6.1. We share personal data only where there is a legitimate reason to do so. Recipients include:

  • Partner Universities and Higher Education Institutions: We share student profiles and verification matrices with admissions offices for the purpose of placement review.
  • Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS): For processing criminal record clearances on professional pathways.
  • Student Loans Company (SLC) / Student Finance England: For verification of funding eligibility.
  • Home Office Identity Service Providers (IDSPs): For identity and Visa validation.
  • IT and Cloud Providers: Secure hosting systems operating under binding data processing agreements.
  • Academic Referees: Contacting named referees for academic verification.

7. Data Retention Periods

7.1. We retain personal data for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected. Our retention schedule is as follows:

  • Student Placement Records: Retained for the duration of our relationship with the student plus 7 years thereafter.
  • Academic Certificates and Transcripts: Retained for the duration of the application cycle plus 3 years.
  • Financial and Commission Records: Minimum 6 years as required by HMRC.
  • Student Profiles (inactive): Where you have not engaged with us for 36 consecutive months, your profile will be securely archived or deleted.

8. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

8.1. You have the following rights in relation to your personal data. To exercise any of these rights, submit a written request to compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk. We will respond within one calendar month of receipt.

  • Right of Access (DSAR): You may request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You may require us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You may request that we delete your personal data.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: You may ask us to suspend processing.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request that your data be provided in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to Object: You may object to processing carried out on the basis of legitimate interests. Objection to direct marketing is absolute.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

8.2. Right to Lodge a Complaint: You may lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at any time (ico.org.uk).

9. International Data Transfers

9.1. We store and process personal data primarily within the United Kingdom. Where any sub-processors operate outside the UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards (such as Adequacy Decisions or International Data Transfer Agreements) are implemented.

10. Automated Decision-Making

10.1. We may use automated processes to perform initial eligibility screenings (for example, verifying UCAS tariff calculations). All final admissions recommendations and placement decisions are made by human consultants.

11. Data Security

11.1. We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, or alteration. All databases are encrypted at rest and in transit.

12. Contact, Complaints & Policy Updates

12.1. For queries or complaints, please contact our Compliance Lead at compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk.

13. Children's Data

13.1. While our services are primarily for adults, we assist 16-17 year olds applying to foundation courses or university entry. All processing of under-18 data is handled with appropriate safeguards and, where required, parent/guardian oversight.

14. Artificial Intelligence (AI) Use

14.1. Kensington Ivy may use AI tools to support internal administrative workflows (e.g. summarizing documents, researching course codes). AI is never used to make final decisions about student eligibility or placements.

15. Sale of Business or Change of Control

15.1. In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, student data may be transferred to the successor entity under binding confidentiality agreements.

16. Links to External Websites

16.1. Our website may contain links to external sites (such as UCAS, SFE, or university portals). We are not responsible for their content or privacy practices.
