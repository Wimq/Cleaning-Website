Statutory Compliance: Equality Act 2010

1. Policy Statement

1.1. Kensington Ivy Ltd ("the Company") is committed to achieving an admissions advisory environment which provides equality of opportunity and freedom from unlawful discrimination on the grounds of race, sex, pregnancy and maternity, marital or civil partnership status, gender reassignment, disability, religion or beliefs, age, or sexual orientation (the "Protected Characteristics" under the Equality Act 2010).

1.2. We actively support Widening Participation (WP) and social mobility in UK Higher Education, ensuring that students from low-participation neighborhoods (LPN), underrepresented communities, and non-traditional educational backgrounds (such as BTECs, T-Levels, and Access to HE) receive equal advice, mapping, and placement opportunities.

1.3. The Company also aims to provide a service that does not discriminate against its university partners and student candidates in the means by which they can access our advisory and placement services.

2. Objectives of this Policy

2.1. To prevent, reduce and stop all forms of unlawful discrimination in higher education applications in line with the Equality Act 2010.

2.2. To ensure that academic profiling and placement mapping are determined on the basis of academic potential, qualifications, and student choice, irrespective of background.

3. Designated Officer

3.1. The Director has overall responsibility for the effective operation of this policy and for ensuring compliance with discrimination law. Day-to-day operational responsibility has been delegated to the Compliance Manager.

4. Admissions and Selection

4.1. The Company will not discriminate unlawfully when making admissions profiling decisions. Recommendations will be based solely on merit, potential, and target course requirements.

4.2. We will not accept instructions from partner universities that indicate an intention to discriminate unlawfully or filter applicants based on Protected Characteristics.

5. Reasonable Adjustments

5.1. The Company assists disabled students and students with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) in securing appropriate reasonable adjustments during their application and enrollment. This includes advising on applications for Disabled Students' Allowance (DSA) and university-specific support plans.

6. Grievances and Discipline

6.1. Students and partners have a right to pursue a complaint concerning discrimination or victimisation via the Company Complaints Procedure.

6.2. Discrimination and victimisation will be treated as serious misconduct and will be dealt with under the Company Disciplinary Procedure.
