1. Our Commitment to Student Welfare

1.1. KENSINGTON IVY LTD operates with a strict commitment to the safeguarding and welfare of student applicants, particularly under-18s on foundation courses and vulnerable adults. Student safety and mental health support are paramount in our advisory practices.

1.2. We align our operations with UK Higher Education welfare guidelines, the Care Act 2014, and Office for Students (OfS) safeguarding standards. Every recommendation and student profiling step is guided by these principles.

1.3. We expect all staff and partner universities to support student safety, maintaining a collaborative duty of care across all admissions channels.

2. Admissions Pre-screening & Audits

2.1. For students applying to professional courses (such as Medicine, Dentistry, Nursing, or Teaching), we support the pre-screening of necessary clearances prior to university introduction:

  • Enhanced DBS Verification: Ensuring applicants are eligible to apply and clear criminal record audits.
  • Identity and Right to Study: Verifying UK/Irish citizenship or checking international Student Visa status.
  • Academic References: Verifying reference sources directly with official institutional domains.
  • Education History: Conducting gaps analysis on transcripts to ensure a transparent profile.

3. Safeguarding in Higher Education

3.1. Higher education institutions maintain dedicated Designated Safeguarding Officers (DSOs) and Student Wellbeing Services. Their role is to manage and oversee student welfare, disability support, and mental health interventions.

3.2. Universities are subject to the Prevent Duty under the Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015, which aims to protect vulnerable young people from radicalization.

4. When to Contact the University Safeguarding Lead

4.1. University wellbeing teams should be contacted whenever a student shows signs of: (a) Mental health crisis or self-harm; (b) Abuse, exploitation, or harassment; (c) Cyberbullying or digital safety threats; (d) Radicalization risk.

4.2. Kensington Ivy will guide applicants to the appropriate student welfare or disability officer at their target university during the admissions transition period.

5. Local Safeguarding Contacts

5.1. Safeguarding issues are escalated to children's services (for under-18s) or adult social services in the local authority where the student lives or studies.
