2. Status of the Company

2.1. Kensington Ivy operates as a higher education admissions advisory and student placement consultancy. We do not act as a university or higher education institution, and we do not award qualifications.

2.2. The Company is not affiliated with any government body, UCAS, or the Student Loans Company (SLC). Our placement services are provided on a commercial, independent basis.

2.3. The Company operates in compliance with:

  • Equality Act 2010
  • Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR
  • Consumer Rights Act 2015
  • Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) Higher Education Guidance
  • Office for Students (OfS) regulatory framework

3. Terms Applicable to Students & Applicants

3.1. No Fees for Standard Placement. The Company does not charge students a fee for standard university placement advisory services. Our commercial model is commission-based, funded by partner institutions upon successful enrollment.

3.2. Key Information Document (KID). Before any placement commitment is made, the Company will provide you with a Key Information Document detailing: the placement services provided; any applicable advisory fees for premium services; your rights to withdraw; and guidance on Student Finance England (SFE) entitlements.

3.3. Student Warranties. You warrant that:

  • All information provided to the Company — including your identity, academic qualifications, and employment history — is accurate, complete, and not misleading;
  • You have disclosed any academic sanctions, plagiarism findings, or prior exclusions from UK educational institutions;
  • You are not subject to any order, prohibition, or restriction that would prevent enrollment at a UK higher education institution;
  • You will immediately notify the Company if your qualification results, right to study status, or personal circumstances change materially during the application process.

3.4. Accuracy of Materials. The Company will not submit false or misleading materials to any Partner Institution on a Student's behalf. Any misrepresentation discovered post-enrollment may be reported to the relevant university registry and may result in termination of the placement relationship.

3.5. Right to Study. Students are responsible for ensuring they hold a valid right to study in the UK (either as a settled UK/Irish national or under a valid Student Visa). The Company can advise but does not act as an immigration legal adviser.

4. Terms Applicable to Partner Universities

4.1. Acceptance of Terms. The receipt and review of any Student admissions file introduced by the Company, or the enrollment of any Student referred by the Company, constitutes the Partner Institution's full and unqualified acceptance of these Terms and the separately agreed Partnership Schedule.

4.2. Provision of Accurate Information. The Partner Institution shall provide the Company with accurate and complete information regarding: course entry requirements; UCAS tariff points needed; fees for international and home students; and any conditional offer conditions.

4.3. Admissions Decisions. The Partner Institution retains full autonomy and final authority over all admissions decisions. The Company does not guarantee that any student will receive an offer or be enrolled.

4.4. CMA Compliance. The Partner Institution shall ensure that all course information, fee data, and entry condition details provided to the Company are accurate and up-to-date in line with CMA consumer protection guidance for higher education.

4.5. Notification of Enrollment. The Partner Institution shall notify the Company of the Enrollment Date and census clearance date of any Student placed by the Company within 14 days of each occurring, to enable accurate commission invoicing.

4.6. Prohibited Re-routing. The Partner Institution shall not re-route a referred Student through a different admissions channel to avoid Placement Commission liability during the Relevant Period.

5. Welfare, Safeguarding & Prevent Duty

5.1. Student Welfare. Both parties are committed to the welfare, mental health, and safety of all student applicants throughout the admissions process.

5.2. Pre-screening for Professional Courses. For professional courses (such as Medicine, Nursing, Teaching, or Social Work), the Company will support the pre-screening of DBS eligibility and fitness-to-practice requirements as requested by the Partner Institution prior to submission.

5.3. Prevent Duty. Both parties shall cooperate to ensure compliance with the Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015 Prevent Duty, and shall report any concerns about student radicalisation to the relevant authority.

6. Widening Participation & Equal Access

6.1. Both parties agree to actively support widening participation in higher education, ensuring students from underrepresented communities, low-participation neighbourhoods (LPN), and non-traditional qualification backgrounds receive fair consideration.

6.2. The Company will not accept instructions from Partner Institutions that indicate an intention to discriminate unlawfully on the basis of any Protected Characteristic under the Equality Act 2010.

7. Direct Application Policy

7.1. A Placement Commission shall remain payable if a Student introduced by the Company is subsequently enrolled directly by the Partner Institution — through any admissions channel — within the Relevant Period of 12 months from initial profile submission.

7.2. If a Student introduced by the Company defers enrollment to a subsequent term or academic year, the Placement Commission remains payable upon successful enrollment in that deferred intake.

8. Commission, Invoicing & Late Payment

8.1. The Company will issue invoices to Partner Institutions following confirmation of a Student's census clearance date.

8.2. Payment of invoices is due within 30 days of the invoice date, unless an alternative payment term has been expressly agreed in writing with a Director of the Company.

8.3. In the event of late payment, the Company reserves the right to charge statutory interest under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 at the rate of 8% per annum above the Bank of England base rate, calculated daily, from the due date until the date of full payment.

8.4. The Company also reserves the right to claim reasonable debt recovery costs in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts Regulations 2013.

8.5. All fees quoted by the Company are exclusive of VAT. VAT will be applied at the prevailing rate where applicable and shown separately on all invoices.
