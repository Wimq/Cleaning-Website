1. Introduction

1.1. This statement is published by KENSINGTON IVY LTD (Companies House No. 17167406, registered office: 453 South End Road, Hornchurch, England, RM12 5NX). It sets out the steps we have taken to ensure that modern slavery and human trafficking are not taking place in our business or supply chains.

1.2. Modern slavery is a crime and a grave violation of fundamental human rights. It encompasses slavery, servitude, forced and compulsory labour, and human trafficking. Kensington Ivy operates a zero-tolerance policy towards any form of modern slavery or exploitation in connection with our business.

1.3. Voluntary Publication: The mandatory reporting obligation under Section 54 of the Modern Slavery Act 2015 applies to commercial organisations with a global annual turnover of £36 million or more. Kensington Ivy does not currently meet this threshold. We publish this statement voluntarily as a demonstration of our ethical standards and commitment to transparency from the outset of our operations.

2. Our Business and Supply Chains

2.1. Kensington Ivy is a university admissions and student placement consultancy. We help UK students secure placements in higher education institutions and partner with universities to manage verified admissions referrals.

2.2. Our primary supply chains consist of:

  • Students / Applicants: Students who register with us seeking academic advisory and university course placements.
  • University Partners: Higher education institutions receiving student applications.
  • Professional Services: Qualification mapping tools (UK ENIC), reference verification systems, and background check providers.
  • Technology Suppliers: Cloud infrastructure, portal databases, and communications tools.

2.3. The higher education sector's strict identity checks, academic qualification audits, and student loan verification protocols serve as controls that substantially reduce modern slavery risk within our operations.

3. Our Policies

3.1. The following policies govern our approach to preventing modern slavery:

  • Zero-Tolerance Policy: No form of modern slavery, forced labour, or human trafficking is tolerated in our business or supply chains. Any breach will result in immediate termination of the relevant relationship and referral to appropriate authorities.
  • Student Verification Policy: All student documents, predicted grades, and references undergo check procedures prior to submission to partner institutions.
  • No Fees Policy: In compliance with UK higher education admissions standards, we do not charge students any fee for standard placement services. This directly reduces the risk of debt exploitation.
  • Whistleblowing Policy: Staff, applicants, and partners may report modern slavery concerns confidentially to compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk.
  • Supplier Code of Conduct: All material suppliers are required to confirm their own compliance with applicable modern slavery obligations and to operate ethical employment practices.

4. Due Diligence

4.1. We apply the following due diligence measures to identify and mitigate modern slavery risks:

  • Mandatory identity verification and academic credential checks for every student applicant using official records and reference checks.
  • Chronological education history reviews, investigating and documenting any gaps to flag potential indicators of exploitation or coercion.
  • Verification of student funding destination accounts to prevent tuition fee diversion or financial exploitation.
  • Direct 1-on-1 consultations with applicants during profiling, conducted away from third-party influence.
  • Pre-engagement due diligence on all material third-party suppliers, reviewing their modern slavery compliance commitments.

5. Risk Assessment

5.1. We assess the risk of modern slavery within our direct operations as low, on the basis that: all candidates are individually verified consenting adults; our mandatory vetting processes function as anti-exploitation controls; and we operate exclusively within the UK's regulated education sector.

5.2. We assess the risk within certain third-party technology supply chains as moderate, and address this through contractual obligations, supplier due diligence, and preference for UK-based or high-standard international providers.

5.3. Our risk assessment will be reviewed annually and updated to reflect any changes in our operations or supply chains.

6. Training & Awareness

6.1. All staff receive modern slavery awareness training at induction and annually thereafter. Training covers: recognition of modern slavery and trafficking indicators; student rights and protections; and internal reporting procedures.

7. Reporting a Concern

7.1. If you suspect modern slavery, human trafficking, or labour exploitation in connection with our business or supply chains, please report it via one of the following channels:

  • Internal (Kensington Ivy): compliance@kensingtonivy.co.uk | 020 7444 0009
  • Modern Slavery Helpline (Unseen UK): 08000 121 700 — free, 24/7, confidential
  • National Referral Mechanism: Via the Home Office Single Competent Authority (England, Wales & Northern Ireland)
  • Police: 999 (emergency) | 101 (non-emergency)

8. Approval

8.1. This statement has been approved by the Director of KENSINGTON IVY LTD and will be reviewed annually.
