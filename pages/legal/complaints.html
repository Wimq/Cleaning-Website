1. Our Commitment

1.1. Kensington Ivy Ltd is committed to providing a high-level service to our student applicants and university partners. If you do not receive satisfaction from us we need you to tell us about it. This will help us to improve our standards.

2. Complaints Procedure

2.1. If you have a complaint, please contact our Compliance Manager in writing (via email to complaints@kensingtonivy.co.uk) outlining the exact nature of your grievance.

3. Next Steps

3.1. We will send you an email acknowledging receipt of your complaint within three working days of receiving it, enclosing a copy of this procedure.

3.2. We will then investigate your complaint. This will normally involve passing your complaint to a designated Director, who will review your file and speak to the advisor who dealt with you.

3.3. The Director will send you a detailed written reply to your complaint, including their suggestions for resolving the matter, within 14 days of sending you the acknowledgement letter.

3.4. If you remain dissatisfied with the outcome, you can write to us again and we will arrange for a different Director or an independent third party to review the decision.

3.5. We will write to you within 14 days of receiving your request for a review, confirming our final position on your complaint and explaining our reasons.

4. Escalation (Higher Education Adjudication)

4.1. If you are still not satisfied after the internal process is complete, you can escalate the matter. If your complaint concerns our partnership practices or university admissions standards, you may contact the Office for Students (OfS) or raise issues directly with the target university's registry. For complaints concerning enrolled student status, the Office of the Independent Adjudicator (OIA) handles higher education grievances.
